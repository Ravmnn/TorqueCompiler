General common expressions you would find in any programming language.


# Unary

- `(x)`: first evaluate the expression inside parenthesis.
- `!x`: invert `x` (boolean).
- `-x`: invert `x` (number).




# Binary

- `x = y`: assigns the value `y` to `x`.
- `x == y`: `x` equals `y`?
- `x != y`: `x` differs from `y`?
- `x > y`: `x` higher than `y`?
- `x < y`: `x` lesser than `y`?
- `x >= y`: `x` higher or equal to `y`?
- `x <= y`: `x` lesser or equal to `y`?
- `x && y`: `x` and `y` are `true`?
- `x || y`: `x` or `y` are `true`?
- `x + y`: addition.
- `x - y`: subtraction.
- `x * y`: multiplication.
- `x / y`: division.
